Has It Surpassed its Original?

Recently, remake of horror movie It has broken the record of The Exorcist as the most popular horror movie. However, the first adaptation of It in the 1990s was not as popular as the remake. One reason for this is that the technology back then could not depict the clown, Pennywise, well. [T]he production emphasizes computer generated imagery. This CGI resulted in the expanding of Pennywise's face...What a thrill, ...claimed student newspaper writer Ben Sefarbi. Since special effects like CGI are used in a lot of horror movies, horror movies are relatively low-budget. A data of horror movie production shows that the production budget has decreased annually. Recent movies had a 70% lower production budget than those released in between 1995-1999. This accounts for the production increase from less than 20 horror movies a year in 1990s to more than sixty in 2017.









Depicts the Reality

In addition to CGI, the wide usage of social media like Facebook, Snapchat, and Twitter also develops topics for horror movies. As Lauren Le Vine critiques, What's most jarring about watching Unfriended is how closely it mirrors your own increasingly screen-timed life (Vine). ...The movie Unfriended demonstrates how cyberbullying would ruin one's life. This movie depicts reality by using video calls on Skype as the basis of communication between characters; moreover, the majority of the movie is filmed on a computer screen. This movie attracted the audience because they can relate themselves to using social medias. According to Social Media Today, an average person spends around two hours on social media each day while teens spend around nine hours (Asano). Therefore, the use of technology leads to the interest in horror movies.











Get Out

Furthermore, horror movies illustrate social issues such as racism. Get Out was ranked second in box office revenue in 2017. Entertainment contributor Stephen Kent stated, Get Out was heralded as a commentary on the unspoken racism that continues to exist in communities all around America (Bukszpan). ...Through this movie, the audience can relate to the issue of racial discrimination when Chris and the other African Americans experienced violent treatments, as their talent and abilities were stolen.
